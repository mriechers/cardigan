## Design Context

### Users
Small team (2-5 editors/producers) at PBS Wisconsin using Cardigan to process video transcripts into SEO metadata. Used during the workday at desks — a production tool, not a consumer product. The interface should respect their time and expertise.

### Brand Personality
**Technical, precise, confident.** Like a well-made instrument — it does its job without drawing attention to itself. PBS Wisconsin branding is present but subtle (logo in nav, brand blue available but not overused).

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Tone**: Inspired by Bear and iA Writer — typography-forward, distraction-free, generous whitespace. Confident restraint over information density.
- **Theme**: Dark mode (production/editorial context, desk work)
- **Anti-references**: Generic SaaS dashboards (too many cards/metrics), flashy startup aesthetics (gradients, glassmorphism, neon), enterprise density (Jira/ServiceNow)

### Design Principles
1. **Show less, mean more** — Every element must earn its place. Progressive disclosure over information overload.
2. **Typography is the interface** — Clear hierarchy through type, not decorative containers. Reduce visual chrome.
3. **Confidence through restraint** — Fewer colors, fewer borders, fewer competing elements. Let whitespace do the work.
4. **Respect expertise** — Users know what they're doing. Don't over-explain, don't add training wheels, don't repeat information.
5. **Instrument, not dashboard** — This is a tool for getting work done, not a status display to admire.
